Feasibility Study

Milk Tea Chain in Pakistan · February 2026

Verdict: GO

Executive Summary

Pakistan presents a high-potential, early-stage market for a milk tea (boba/bubble tea) chain. The country is the world's largest tea importer ($611M in 2023), has a median age of ~21 years, and a deep-rooted chai culture that creates natural familiarity with tea-based beverages.

As of early 2025, only ~31 bubble tea stores exist across the country — a 14.8% increase from 2023 — signaling growing demand but low saturation. The global bubble tea market is growing at 8–9.5% CAGR and is projected to reach $5–9B by 2032–2035.

Verdict: Feasible with strong upside, but requires careful localization, cost management, and phased rollout.

Pakistan is the world's #1 tea importer. Tea ("chai") is not just a beverage — it is a social ritual embedded in daily life across all income levels. This deep cultural affinity for tea is a massive advantage for a milk tea chain, as the product doesn't need to be introduced from scratch.

Primary target: Urban millennials and Gen Z (ages 15–35) in Tier 1 cities — Lahore, Karachi, Islamabad/Rawalpindi.

Secondary target: University students, young professionals, and social-media-active consumers.

1.3 Consumer Behavior Drivers

  • Social media influence: TikTok drives 74% of consumer familiarity with bubble tea globally. Pakistan's TikTok user base is among the largest in South Asia.
  • Café culture boom: The past decade has seen explosive growth in cafés and casual dining in Pakistani cities.
  • Customization appeal: Young consumers value personalized drinks (sweetness levels, ice levels, toppings).
  • Instagrammability: Visually appealing boba drinks drive organic social sharing.

2. Competitive Landscape

2.1 Current Market State

As of January 2025, there are only 31 bubble tea stores across all of Pakistan:

  • Punjab: 15 stores
  • Sindh: 12 stores
  • Khyber Pakhtunkhwa: 3 stores
  • Other: 1 store

This is an extremely low number for a 240M+ population, indicating the market is in its infancy.

4.2 Localization — Key Differentiator

The desi-fusion category is critical for Pakistan. Blending familiar chai flavors (cardamom, cinnamon, masala) with the boba format creates a product that:

  • Feels familiar yet novel
  • Differentiates from international brands
  • Justifies pricing vs. street chai

7.3 Compliance Risks

  • Penalty for non-registration: Up to 1 year imprisonment + PKR 500,000 fine
  • Food authorities conduct regular inspections — maintain hygiene standards
  • Halal compliance is essential (all ingredients must be halal-certified)
  • If importing ingredients, you'll need an import license and must clear customs duties
